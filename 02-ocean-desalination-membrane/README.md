## The Problem

Desalination provides 1% of global drinking water but uses 75 million kWh/day. Reverse osmosis needs 3-5 kWh/m³ and high-pressure pumps (60-80 bar). In developing coastal countries, desalinated water costs $1-2/m³ — too expensive for most communities.

## The Solution

Superconducting coils create a magnetic field gradient that separates salt ions from water. The Lorentz force pushes ions to one side while water passes through. No pressure needed — just magnetic fields.

**How it works:**
1. Seawater enters superconducting channel
2. Magnetic field gradient applies Lorentz force to ions
3. Ions deflected to waste stream
4. Fresh water exits clean side
5. No pressure, no membranes to foul
6. Solar-powered

**Components:**
- RTS coil array (gradient field)
- Flow channel (non-magnetic material)
- Ion collection waste stream
- Solar panel + battery
- Standard water treatment (UV, filtration)

**Novel contribution:** Magnetic desalination eliminates the high-pressure pumps that make RO energy-intensive. 0.3 kWh/m³ vs 3-5 kWh/m³.
