## The Problem

300 million people rely on groundwater that's being depleted. Finding new aquifers requires expensive drilling ($10,000-50,000 per well). 70% of wells in Africa go dry because they're drilled in the wrong place. Traditional geophysical surveys cost $50,000+ and take months.

## The Solution

Superconducting sensors detect the magnetic signature of underground water. Water has a distinct magnetic susceptibility that differs from rock, soil, and air. The sensor array maps subsurface water distribution in real-time.

**How it works:**
1. Sensor array deployed on surface (100m x 100m grid)
2. RTS SQUID sensors measure local magnetic field
3. Water body creates detectable anomaly
4. AI processing maps 3D water distribution
5. Optimal drilling location identified
6. Drilling success rate: 95%+

**Components:**
- RTS SQUID sensor array (100 nodes)
- Data acquisition system
- AI processing unit (laptop-scale)
- GPS positioning
- Solar power

**Novel contribution:** Room-temperature SQUID sensors make field-deployable water detection practical. Current magnetotelluric surveys cost $50K+. This costs $5K.
