## The Problem

Rivers and lakes in developing countries are contaminated by agricultural runoff, industrial waste, and sewage. 3 million people die annually from water pollution. Building water treatment plants costs millions and takes years. Floating treatment systems exist but need frequent maintenance.

## The Solution

A superconducting coil generates magnetic fields that attract and aggregate contaminants (metals, bacteria, microplastics). The device floats on water, filters continuously, and sterilizes using pulsed magnetic fields.

**How it works:**
1. Device floats on water surface
2. Magnetic field attracts metal ions and charged particles
3. Contaminants aggregated in collection chamber
4. Pulsed field sterilizes biological contaminants
5. Clean water released below device
6. Solar-powered, autonomous operation

**Components:**
- RTS coil array (attraction + sterilization)
- Floating platform (buoyant material)
- Collection chamber (removable)
- Solar panel + battery
- GPS + telemetry (monitoring)

**Novel contribution:** Combines magnetic attraction and sterilization in one autonomous floating device. No consumables. Drop it in, forget it.
