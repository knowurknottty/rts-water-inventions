## The Problem

Glaciers are retreating at record rates. The Hindu Kush Himalayas provide water for 2 billion people. By 2100, 2/3 of glacial ice could be gone. Meltwater currently flows into the ocean — wasted. Capturing it requires expensive dams and pipelines.

## The Solution

Superconducting coils create magnetic field gradients that guide meltwater flow. Water (being slightly diamagnetic) can be directed using field gradients. The device captures meltwater at the glacier edge and channels it into underground storage.

**How it works:**
1. Device positioned at glacier terminus
2. Magnetic field gradients guide meltwater flow
3. Water channeled into underground aquifer recharge
4. No dam, no pipeline, no infrastructure
5. Solar-powered, autonomous
6. Monitors glacier retreat in real-time

**Components:**
- RTS coil array (field gradients)
- Flow channel system
- Underground injection well
- Solar panel + battery
- GPS + glacier monitoring sensors

**Novel contribution:** Magnetic guidance of water flow is a new application. Eliminates the need for physical infrastructure (dams, pipelines) that disrupts ecosystems.
