## The Problem

Wastewater treatment costs $500B/year globally. In developing countries, 80% of wastewater is discharged untreated. The nutrients (nitrogen, phosphorus) in wastewater cause algal blooms and dead zones. Meanwhile, fertilizer costs are rising and phosphorus reserves are depleting.

## The Solution

Superconducting coils generate magnetic fields that selectively extract ions from wastewater. Different minerals have different magnetic susceptibilities and can be separated sequentially. The result: clean water + concentrated mineral fertilizer.

**How it works:**
1. Wastewater enters superconducting channel
2. Sequential magnetic fields extract different ions
3. Nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium concentrated
4. Heavy metals (lead, mercury) extracted separately
5. Clean water exits
6. Mineral concentrate sold as fertilizer

**Components:**
- RTS coil array (multiple field strengths)
- Ion-selective channels
- Collection chambers (per mineral type)
- Power electronics
- Solar panel

**Novel contribution:** Magnetic ion separation in wastewater is demonstrated at lab scale. RTS enables continuous-flow industrial deployment. Revenue from fertilizer offsets operating costs.
